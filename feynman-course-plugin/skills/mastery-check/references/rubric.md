# Mastery Check Scoring Rubric

This rubric defines how to score the 5-question mastery check that concludes each Feynman-style course. The mastery check tests whether the learner truly understood the material, not just whether they memorized facts.

---

## Question 1 - Recall (20 points)

Tests whether the learner can accurately remember the core concept.

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **20** | Accurate, complete recall of the key concept. Includes the essential details without prompting. |
| **10** | Partially correct, missing important details. Gets the gist but omits a critical element or includes a minor inaccuracy. |
| **0** | Incorrect or unable to recall. Either states something wrong or cannot produce an answer. |

---

## Question 2 - Explain Simply (25 points)

Tests whether the learner can explain the concept in plain language (the Feynman test).

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **25** | Clear, accurate explanation a non-expert would understand. Uses appropriate analogies or examples. No unnecessary jargon. Logical flow from start to finish. |
| **15** | Mostly clear but uses jargon or skips key steps. A non-expert would need follow-up questions. The core idea is present but the explanation has gaps. |
| **5** | Vague or contains significant errors. The learner has a rough sense of the idea but cannot articulate it coherently. |
| **0** | Unable to explain. Cannot produce a meaningful explanation. |

---

## Question 3 - Application (25 points)

Tests whether the learner can apply the concept to a novel scenario they haven't seen before.

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **25** | Correctly applies concept to the scenario with reasoning. Shows their work: identifies which concept applies, explains why, and arrives at the right conclusion. |
| **15** | Right direction but incomplete reasoning. Picks the right concept but doesn't fully explain the connection, or gets most of the way to the answer but misses a step. |
| **5** | Attempts application but significant errors. Tries to use the right concept but applies it incorrectly, or picks the wrong concept entirely but shows some understanding. |
| **0** | Cannot apply. Does not attempt application or produces an answer unrelated to the concepts taught. |

---

## Question 4 - Misconception Trap (20 points)

Presents a plausible-sounding but false statement. Tests whether the learner can identify and debunk common misconceptions.

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **20** | Correctly identifies the false statement AND explains why it's wrong. Demonstrates understanding of the underlying truth that the misconception distorts. |
| **10** | Identifies it's false but can't explain why. Has the right intuition but lacks the depth to articulate the reasoning. |
| **0** | Falls for the misconception. Accepts the false statement as true, indicating a gap in understanding. |

---

## Question 5 - Confidence Rating (10 points)

The learner rates their own confidence level on a 1-5 scale. This tests metacognitive calibration - do they know what they know?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **10** | Self-assessment aligns with actual performance (within 1 level). The learner accurately gauges their own understanding. |
| **5** | Slightly miscalibrated (off by 2 levels). The learner is somewhat over- or under-confident relative to their performance. |
| **0** | Significantly miscalibrated. The learner's self-assessment is wildly off from their actual performance (e.g., rates 5/5 confidence but scores below 50%). |

**Confidence-to-performance mapping**:
- Confidence 5 = expects 90-100 points
- Confidence 4 = expects 70-89 points
- Confidence 3 = expects 50-69 points
- Confidence 2 = expects 30-49 points
- Confidence 1 = expects 0-29 points

---

## Total Score: 100 points

### Mastery Levels

| Score Range | Level | Meaning | Recommended Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| **90-100** | Mastery achieved | Ready to teach this topic to others. Deep understanding with accurate self-awareness. | Celebrate. Optionally generate an advanced follow-up course. |
| **70-89** | Strong understanding | Solid grasp with minor gaps. May struggle with edge cases or nuance. | Review the specific lessons connected to missed questions. Provide targeted clarification. |
| **50-69** | Developing | Foundational understanding is forming but significant gaps remain. Concepts are not yet connected into a coherent whole. | Revisit the lessons where gaps appeared. Re-read the analogies and examples. Retake the mastery check after review. |
| **Below 50** | Needs significant review | The core concepts have not landed. The learner may need a different approach or more foundational prerequisites. | Restart from the beginning with adjusted pacing. Consider whether prerequisites are missing. Offer a simpler entry point if available. |

---

## Scoring Guidelines for Course Generators

- Questions should be generated based on the actual course content, not generic topic knowledge.
- Question 3 (Application) must use a scenario NOT covered in any lesson. The learner must transfer knowledge.
- Question 4 (Misconception Trap) should use a misconception identified during the diagnose-and-map phase.
- The confidence rating (Question 5) should be asked BEFORE the learner sees their results, to avoid anchoring.
- When reporting results, show the score for each question individually so the learner knows exactly where their gaps are.
